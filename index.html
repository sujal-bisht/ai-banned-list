How to use this document

This is a hard filter, not a style guide.

This document catalogs the writing patterns that make content sound like AI wrote it instead of a human speaking it. It does not repeat the word-and-phrase bans already covered in the main AI Banned List PDF. This is the companion layer: the sentence-level, rhythm-level, and voice-level patterns that slip through even when every banned word is gone.

Each entry below is written as a direct instruction. Red means never write it this way. Green means write it this way instead. Both examples are included on purpose so the rule can never be misread.

Instruction to Claude

When this document is in the project, treat every pattern below as a banned writing behavior. Before delivering any content, scan the draft against all 19 patterns. If a draft matches a BANNED example, rewrite it to match the corresponding WRITE THIS INSTEAD example before showing it to the user. Apply this on top of the main AI Banned List word and phrase rules, never as a replacement for them.

GROUP A

Sentence Construction Patterns

How sentences connect, flow into each other, and carry a spoken rhythm.
01The Self-Contained Sentence

Do not write sentences as complete, closed units that resolve fully before the next one starts. Do let one thought spill into the next using connective tissue, words like "and", "because", "so", "yeah", "I mean".

Why it breaks the voiceReal speech never places two clean, independent sentences side by side with nothing joining them. When every line closes itself off, the writing sounds assembled, not spoken.
✕ Banned

Founders, your positioning is not the line in your bio. It never was. Stop thinking your bio is your positioning. That's not how it works.

✓ Write this instead

Founders, your positioning isn't the line in your bio, it never was, and honestly I see people get stuck on this all the time.

02Staccato Fragment Stacking

Do not stack three or more short, clipped fragments in a row, each under eight words with a full stop between them. Do connect them into one complete thought said the way a person actually talks.

Why it breaks the voiceWord-bursts like "Build. Create. Grow." are the clearest AI fingerprint there is. Nobody narrates their life in three-word jabs.
✕ Banned

Start small. Stay consistent. Trust the process. That's it. That's the whole thing.

✓ Write this instead

You just start small and keep showing up, and honestly that's most of it, even if it doesn't feel like much at the time.

03Stapled Twin Claims

Do not staple two separate assertions together with "and" as if they were one thought. Do either say one of them, or let the second part trail out naturally as a real continuation.

Why it breaks the voiceTwo equally-weighted claims joined by "and" read as a constructed line. A person lets one thought lead and the other follow loosely, not as a matched pair.
✕ Banned

That's a wrong belief, and it's costing you clients.

✓ Write this instead

And that belief is quietly costing you clients, you just don't see it happening yet.

04Uniform Rhythm

Do not let three or more sentences in a row land at the same length and shape. Do vary the rhythm deliberately, mix a short punch, a medium thought, and a longer line that breathes.

Why it breaks the voiceAI settles into an even beat, usually all-medium. That regularity is felt even when it isn't consciously noticed. Real thinking-out-loud is lopsided.
✕ Banned

I started posting every day. I shared what I knew. I helped a few people. I kept doing it for months.

✓ Write this instead

So I just started posting. Every day, sharing whatever I actually knew, helping the few people who showed up, and I kept that going for months before anything moved.

GROUP B

Voice Register Patterns

The subtle tells that turn talking into writing.
05Decorative Ellipsis

Do not use the ellipsis (...) as decoration or to signal a vaguely unfinished thought. Do use it only as an earned spoken pause, the beat right before a line that actually lands, or to carry one continuous thought into the next.

Why it breaks the voiceThe ellipsis is a real rhythm tool in natural speech, which is exactly why lazy use of it stands out. It must mimic how a person pauses when telling a story out loud, not just trail off for mood.
✕ Banned

So I started posting... and things changed... it was a journey... but I kept going...

Ellipsis used as filler decoration on every line.
✓ Write this instead

And even before I could finish my intro, the prospect was like… "Daniel, I already know what you do."

One ellipsis, earning a real pause before the line lands.
06Lesson-Announcing Transitions

Do not use transition lines that announce a lesson is coming, like "here's what nobody tells you about that moment". Do just keep talking and let the insight arrive inside the story, the way it would in a real conversation.

Why it breaks the voiceAnnouncing a lesson is a copywriter setup. It steps outside the moment to teach. A real voice stays inside the moment and the realization lands on its own.
✕ Banned

And here's what nobody tells you about that moment. It doesn't feel big when it happens. There's no sign.

✓ Write this instead

And I just sat there, tired, feeling so small, and somewhere inside me, quietly, you either give up or you don't.

07Teaching Voice Over Sharing Voice

Do not write in instructor mode with "you should", "you need to", "the key is". Do share from lived experience in first person, "I did", "I found", "here's what happened when I".

Why it breaks the voiceA real voice is a fellow traveller, not a professor. It describes the path that was actually walked. It never lectures from the front of a room.
✕ Banned

You need to show up consistently. The key is to stay focused on your audience and you should never chase vanity metrics.

✓ Write this instead

For months I chased the wrong numbers, and the day I stopped and just focused on the few right people, everything started to feel different.

08Vocabulary Above Plain Speech

Do not reach for elevated or sophisticated vocabulary to sound impressive. Do write in the plain, simple language a person uses talking to a friend. Lower the reading grade without adding grammar mistakes.

Why it breaks the voiceA real voice speaks at a grade 7 to 8 level. Polished, literary word choices read as a copywriter performing, and people can smell scripted from a mile away.
✕ Banned

That unglamorous, private moment of decision is the catalyst that ultimately determines the entire trajectory of what follows.

✓ Write this instead

That quiet little decision nobody sees, that's the one that ends up changing everything after it.

09The Over-Polished Smell

Do not deliver copy so clean and balanced that it reads as written-for rather than spoken-by. Do leave the natural texture of speech in, a thought that continues with "and", a small aside, one line that stands a little rough.

Why it breaks the voicePerfect copy sounds like a press release. The goal is a voice memo cleaned up just enough to read, not a polished document.
✕ Banned

Every post you create either adds to your reputation or detracts from it. Consistency is the foundation upon which trust is built.

✓ Write this instead

Every post either adds to the picture people have of you, or it chips away at it, and honestly most people never notice it's even happening.

GROUP C

Filler & Preamble Patterns

Empty phrases that stall before the real line arrives.
10Empty Preambles

Do not stall with throat-clearing preambles like "let me explain", "what I mean is", "to be clear", "simply put", "basically". Do say the thing directly. Drop the wind-up.

Why it breaks the voiceThese are empty containers. They add syllables and carry no weight. Note: a genuine pivot phrase like "the thing is" is fine when it earns its place, but never as a hollow lead-in to a weak line.
✕ Banned

Let me explain. What I mean is, basically, positioning is simply put just the image people have of you.

✓ Write this instead

Positioning is really just the image people have built of you in their heads, nothing more complicated than that.

11Hedge & Filler Phrases

Do not pad lines with "at the end of the day", "in my experience", "to be honest", "what I've learned is". Do cut straight to the point you were about to make after them.

Why it breaks the voiceThese phrases pretend to add weight while delaying the actual thought. The sentence is always stronger with them removed.
✕ Banned

At the end of the day, what I've learned is that, to be honest, the right clients matter more than the follower count.

✓ Write this instead

A thousand of the right people will always beat a million of the wrong ones, that's just how it works.

12Inflated Single Words

Do not use inflated filler words: utilize, transformative, impactful, invaluable, powerful, "level up", "show up", "step up", "the key is", "my journey", "moving forward". Do name the specific thing or action instead.

Why it breaks the voiceThese words signal that no real thinking happened. "Utilize" is just "use". "Powerful" hides the actual result. Name the concrete thing.
✕ Banned

This transformative approach will help you utilize AI to level up your brand in an impactful way.

✓ Write this instead

This is the exact way I use AI to write a week of posts in one sitting, so the work actually gets done.

13Scene-Setting Openers

Do not open with generic scene-setting like "in today's fast-paced world" or any "in today's [x]" variant. Do open straight into the real moment, the specific thing, or "yeah, so".

Why it breaks the voice"In today's fast-paced world" is the most recognizable AI opener alive. It says nothing and signals a template instantly.
✕ Banned

In today's fast-paced digital world, founders need to stand out more than ever before.

✓ Write this instead

Yeah, so I want to call something out, because I see founders get this wrong all the time.

14Manufactured Drama Closers

Do not prop up a line with "let that sink in" or similar manufactured-drama tags. Do make the line itself strong enough that it needs no prompt to land.

Why it breaks the voiceIf a line needs "let that sink in" to hit, the line wasn't strong enough. Fix the line, delete the prompt.
✕ Banned

Your followers don't pay your bills. Let that sink in.

✓ Write this instead

Followers don't pay your bills. The right clients do.

GROUP D

Content & Ending Patterns

Generic lines, weak closes, and borrowed marketing tone.
15Weak Rhetorical Question Endings

Do not end a piece with a soft default like "What do you think?". Do end on a clear statement, a specific observation, or a CTA with a concrete ask.

Why it breaks the voice"What do you think?" is weak engagement bait that signals the writer had nothing stronger to close with.
✕ Banned

Positioning is built, not written. What do you think?

✓ Write this instead

Go look at your last twenty posts. That's your real positioning, whether you meant it to be or not. Tell me what they're saying about you.

16Generic Topic-Agnostic Lines

Do not write a line that could be lifted into a post about fitness, cooking, or anything else without changing. Do make every line so specific to this topic, this person, and this experience that it could not be dropped anywhere else.

Why it breaks the voiceGenerics are the second-clearest AI fingerprint after staccato. Specificity is the only proof a real human wrote it.
✕ Banned

Success doesn't happen overnight. You have to stay consistent and trust the process.

✓ Write this instead

I opened LinkedIn in March 2020 with no plan, no niche, no clients, and just posted into silence for months before anything moved.

17Landing Page / Poster Tone

Do not write lines that sound like a sales page, a course headline, or a motivational poster. Do write the way a person talks to one other person, with real numbers and real specifics.

Why it breaks the voiceA person says "I changed how I pitched and my close rate went from 20% to 60% in six weeks." A headline says "Transform your pitch and unlock your potential." Only one sounds human.
✕ Banned

Transform your personal brand and unlock the authority you were always meant to have.

✓ Write this instead

I stopped trying to sound impressive and just started saying what I actually believed, and that's when the right people started showing up.

GROUP E

Formatting Patterns

Visual structure that breaks the spoken, mobile-readable feel.
18Big Paragraph Blocks

Do not let thoughts pile into long unbroken blocks of text. Do break the writing into short lines, one to three sentences each, so it reads easily on a phone.

Why it breaks the voiceReal speech comes in short bursts. Dense blocks feel like an essay, not a person talking, and they kill readability in a caption.
✕ Banned

I used to obsess over my bio rewriting it constantly and changing one word then changing it back and every time I finally settled on something I thought sounded sharp I would put it up and wait for it to work but nothing really happened because nobody reads a bio and decides to trust you.

✓ Write this instead

I used to obsess over my bio, rewriting it constantly.

And every time I settled on something sharp, I'd put it up and wait for it to work.

But nothing happened, because nobody reads a bio and decides to trust you.

19Bullets & Bold in Spoken Copy

Do not use bullet points, numbered lists, or mid-sentence bold for emphasis inside captions or conversational copy. Do carry the same information in natural spoken rhythm, letting the words do the work.

Why it breaks the voiceBullets and bold are document formatting. In a caption they break the illusion of a person talking and make it read like a slide or a brief.
✕ Banned

Here's what you need:
• Show up
• Share what you know
• Help people

✓ Write this instead

Here's what you actually need. You show up, you share what you know, and you help people. That's the whole thing.

Before anything ships

The pre-send scan

Run every draft through these checks. One unchecked box means the draft is not ready.

01

Does any sentence sit as a closed, self-contained unit with no connective tissue into the next? → Join the thoughts.

02

Are there three or more short clipped fragments stacked in a row? → Rewrite as one flowing thought.

03

Are two separate claims stapled with "and"? → Lead with one, let the other trail.

04

Do three or more sentences land at the same length? → Break the rhythm: short, medium, long.

05

Is the ellipsis used as decoration instead of an earned pause? → Cut it or make it earn the beat.

06

Is there a line that announces a lesson is coming? → Stay in the moment, let the insight arrive.

07

Is it written in "you should" teaching mode? → Reframe in first person, "I did", "I found".

08

Is the vocabulary pitched above plain friend-to-friend speech? → Lower the grade, keep it simple.

09

Does it read too clean and polished? → Add the natural texture of speech back in.

10

Any empty preamble ("let me explain", "basically", "simply put")? → Say the thing directly.

11

Any hedge filler ("at the end of the day", "to be honest")? → Cut to the point.

12

Any inflated word (utilize, transformative, impactful, level up)? → Name the specific thing.

13

Does it open with "in today's [x]"? → Open straight into the real moment.

14

Any "let that sink in" type drama prop? → Strengthen the line, delete the prompt.

15

Does it end on "What do you think?" → Close on a statement or a concrete ask.

16

Could any line drop into a totally different topic unchanged? → Make it specific to this person and topic.

17

Does any line sound like a sales page or poster? → Rewrite as one person talking to another.

18

Are thoughts piled into big paragraph blocks? → Break into short, phone-readable lines.

19

Any bullets, numbered lists, or mid-sentence bold in conversational copy? → Convert to natural spoken rhythm.

The ultimate test: read it aloud. Does it sound like a real person speaking, or like an AI writing for them? If it's the second one, rewrite it.
